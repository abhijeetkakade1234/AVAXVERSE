## 2024-03-10 - [Denial of Service (DoS) in Escrow Dispute Resolution]
**Vulnerability:** Found a DoS vulnerability in `Escrow.sol`'s `resolveDisputeSplit` function. The function performed direct `.call{value: amount}('')` transfers to multiple parties (`feeRecipient`, `client`, and `freelancer`) and reverted if any transfer failed (`require(ok, 'Escrow: payout failed')`). If any party was a smart contract that rejected ETH, they could block the entire resolution process for everyone.
**Learning:** Even though `_safeTransfer` (which uses a pull-over-push fallback) was implemented and used elsewhere in the contract, it was missed in `resolveDisputeSplit`. Multi-party distributions must always gracefully handle failed transfers to prevent single points of failure.
**Prevention:** Always utilize a pull-over-push pattern for multi-party fund distribution. Use internal helpers like `_safeTransfer` that record failed transfers as `pendingWithdrawals` rather than reverting the entire transaction.
